What is estate planning?
Estate planning is the process of arranging for the management and distribution of your assets during your lifetime and after death. It includes wills, trusts, powers of attorney, healthcare directives, and guardianship designations.
How much does estate planning cost?
Costs vary based on the complexity of your situation. A basic will may start at a few hundred dollars, while comprehensive trust-based plans range from $2,000 to $5,000+. We offer transparent pricing with no hidden fees.
When should I start planning?
The best time to start is now. Whether you're newly married, starting a family, building a business, or entering retirement, having a plan in place gives you and your loved ones peace of mind at every stage.
What's the difference between a will and a trust?
A will takes effect after death and goes through probate. A trust can take effect immediately, avoids probate, and offers greater privacy and flexibility for managing your assets during life and beyond.
Do I need to update my estate plan?
Yes — life changes like marriage, divorce, births, deaths, major purchases, or moves to a new state should trigger a review. We recommend reviewing your plan every 3–5 years at minimum.
